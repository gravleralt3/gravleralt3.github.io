When should I apply to change my degree program or campus?
Application deadlines vary by program. For more information please visit Vancouver Academic Calendar or Okanagan Academic Calendar.
Do I need to submit any other applications or documents?
Some programs have additional requirements, such as supplemental applications, portfolios or auditions. Please check your online application status on the Student Service Centre (SSC) shortly after you apply to see if you need to submit additional information.
 Where do I submit my application to change my degree program?
If you are a current student and would like to change your degree program or campus location, complete the "Change of Degree Program or Campus" application in the "Registration" menu.

If you previously attended UBC and are not currently registered, you can apply for readmission through the Student Service Centre (SSC) under the "Admissions" menu.
 Can I change my personal profile after my application is submitted?
No, once you have submitted your application, it cannot be changed or amended. Please review your responses carefully before submitting your application.
 Who will read my responses?
Your responses may be read by one or more of your program choices.
 Can I work on my responses outside of the application form?

Your responses may be drafted in another format then pasted into the application form. Benefits include the use of grammar, spell check and word count features. Responses that exceed the maximum word count will be cut-off when that maximum is exceeded.

Before leaving this page, be sure to save your responses and note the process to return and complete your application to UBC by the application deadline.

 How do I save my responses?

Regularly click the Save button at the bottom of the page to save your work as you enter responses.

You may access this form, make changes to it and press the Save button as often as you like before you submit your application to UBC or until the application deadline has passed. Once you have completed the whole application and paid your application fee, you will not be able to change your responses. Please review your responses carefully before you submit them.

The Next button takes you to the next part of the application process.

 What if the application deadline passes before I submit my application?
Incomplete applications that have not been submitted and paid for prior to the application deadline will not be considered.
 What are my options to pay the application fee if I do not have a credit card?
Domestic applicants can pay by cheque and international applicants, can pay with a bank draft in Canadian funds. Please send your payment to Undergraduate Admissions, 2016-1874 East Mall, Vancouver BC, V6T 1Z1. Include your full name and date of birth. Your payment must be received by the application deadline. Your application will be considered incomplete until we have received and processed your payment.
 How do I self-identify if I am an Aboriginal person of Canada but not a Canadian citizen?

If you are an Aboriginal person of Canada, but not a Canadian citizen please contact

Vancouver Campus:

Your assigned Enrolment Services Advisor. If you do not have an assigned Enrolment Services Advisor, you may contact an Enrolment Services Advisor using one of the contact options (phone or webform) listed on the Enrolment Services Advisor webpage

Okanagan Campus:

Student Records & Financial Services by email at: srfs.recruitment@ubc.ca or by phone at 250-807-9100.

 Why is this information important and how will it be used?
This information is important because it helps us help you. UBC has a number of unique programs and services specifically for Indigenous students. These programs and services are designed to help support you during your studies at the University such as scholarships and bursaries designated for Indigenous students.
 Who will have access to this information and how will it be protected?
UBC staff and faculty members who have a role in providing Indigenous students with student services will have access to this information. It is protected by University policies governing the use of student information, in particular Policy SC14: Information Systems Policy and the Information Security Standard . It also has legal protection through the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act [RSBC 1996] Chapter 165.
