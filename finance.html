 Where does my money go when I pay student fees?
A detailed breakdown of student fees can be found in the UBC Vancouver Calendar or the UBC Okanagan Calendar.
 What methods of payment does UBC accept?
Interac Online: Allows you to pay with your bank card through your Student Service Centre.

Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT): Allows you to pay fees online, directly from your bank account. To get started, enter your bank account information on the Banking Details page.

Online/telephone banking: Register with your bank to pay your UBC fees like any other bill. Your account number at UBC is your student number.

International Funds Transfer: Allows you to pay with the foreign currency of your choice through your Student Service Centre.

Credit card payment: Allows you to pay by credit card. Please note: Payments for student housing, mandatory meal plans, tuition, assessment fees, late fees, early arrival and extension fees, will have a non-refundable 1.75% convenience fee added to the base payment.

 When will my payment appear on my credit card or bank account?
Interac Online payments appear within minutes, while Electronic Fund Transfer (EFT) and credit card payments may take a few days.
 Where can I find a breakdown of all my course fees?
On your Financial Summary, select the description for the appropriate tuition instalment charge.
 I made a payment on the SSC but I got a time-out message. What should I do?
Wait 48 hours to see whether your payment appears in your Financial Summary. If your fee is due within that 48 hour time frame, please contact us at 604.822.9836 or 1.877.272.1422.
 What is an Awards Refund?
An Awards Refund means that a cheque has issued for you. Because you have already paid your fees, or your total award money exceeds the amount of fees that you owe, you will receive a cheque. The amount that you will receive is the negative (-) amount listed in the Payments column. You will receive an email when your cheque is ready for pickup at Enrolment Services Advising Centre.
 What if I don't pay my tuition and fees by the deadline?
If you have fees that havent been paid by the payment due dates you may be placed on financial hold. For more information and resources visit our late payment of fees page for the Vancouver campus or Okanagan campus.
 How do I get a credit refunded from my tuition account?
If you have a credit on your financial account:
  • Vancouver campus: Request a refund by contacting your Enrolment Services Advisor. If you don't have an ES Advisor, please contact Enrolment Services directly.

  • Okanagan campus: Contact Student Services.
Any credit you have is automatically applied to your future tuition and student fee assessments.
If you are no longer a UBC student, you can search for your name in the Unclaimed Tuition Credit Portal and follow the instructions provided to request a refund.
